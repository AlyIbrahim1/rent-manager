## Design Context

### Users
Property managers and landlords in a professional office/desk context. They manage multiple rental units and need a clear, confident overview of rent collection status, overdue balances, and payment history. Task-focused: they arrive knowing what they need to do.

### Brand Personality
**The Architectural Editor** — authoritative, organized, refined. Three words: *structured*, *precise*, *elevated*. The interface should feel less like a database and more like a premium wealth management tool.

### Aesthetic Direction
"The Curated Ledger" — light mode (professional desk context). Deep navy/charcoal primary (#0f172a, #131b2e) with an emerald tertiary accent (#85f8c4 on #002114) reserved for high-signal CTAs like "Receive Payment." Structural depth through tonal surface layering (surface → surface-container → surface-container-lowest), never with 1px rule lines. Manrope for display/headings, Inter for body/UI.

**Anti-references**: No glassmorphism-everywhere, no glowing cyan on dark, no crowded data-tables with divider lines between every row.

### Design Principles
1. **No-line rule**: Structural boundaries defined by tonal shifts (e.g. surface-container-low → surface-container-lowest), not 1px borders.
2. **Emerald is a beacon**: The tertiary/emerald only appears on one or two primary actions per screen — never as decoration.
3. **Status chips are pills**: `rounded-full` for status chips (Paid, Overdue), `rounded-sm` for category/identifier labels (Apt #).
4. **Soft lift over hard shadow**: Cards use ambient `box-shadow: 0 12px 40px rgba(25,28,30,0.06)` and tonal layering, not standard drop shadows.
5. **Primary buttons use gradient**: Gradient from `primary` (#0f172a) to `primary-container` (#131b2e) for the "weighted" feel.
